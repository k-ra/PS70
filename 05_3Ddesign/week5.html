2D Imagination

Given how joyfully open-ended the prompt was, I spent some time thinking over my infinite options! These are some potential future projects that I was able to sketch up:

1. 3D Printed fashion element, a la current tech-fashion trends. I'm inspired by Iris Van Herpen, who uses organic material as inspiration to 3D print textiles and flexible forms on the human body. I'm also inspired by some of Zac Posen's "husks", almost, where the entire dress is 3d printed and static, instead of in motion. I find the doll-like rigidity of his dresses quite funny and pleasing. I'd like to move through life as if I've just been excavated out of an ice sculpture. The sketches I did were quite elementary; one was just to figure out the shape of a dress, and the other was an embodiment of the sparkle emoji in a two-piece: ✨.

2. Creative stand, for my toothbrush, my pen, or any other object that wishes to stand on a fancier pedestal than it has currently. I imagined a Greek Ionic stand, or an opening flower with petals canting outwards.
3. Ivory sphere-within-a-sphere, inspired by this ancient feat of carving with lace pearls within spheres, all carved out of one block of ivory. This piece is currently held at the National Palace Museum in Taipei. 3D printing this, in comparison, would be easy! However, it would mostly be a feat for imagining what printing support structures would be easiest to excavate!

Chosen Project: Opera Headpiece

The one I settled on was the Peking Opera Headpiece, inspired by the feathers and bobbles attached to the incredibly intricate and dynamic costumes in Peking Opera!
